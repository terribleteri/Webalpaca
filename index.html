Alpacas of Sheffield:

An illustrated adventure by Laura Turpin, Josie Levay and Teri Limongi
ONE

Sheffield is better known for the metallurgic industry but, over the years, the "steel city" and its inhabitants have turned to different and very unexpected activities.

Alpaca farming, as surprising as it may seem, is one of them and it is getting more and more popular.

These very social creatures have brought joy to the people of Sheffield in the past decade as two alpaca farms developed in the area.

Alpacas are gentle and most often regarded as "cuddly" but this exotic pet also produces one of the finest, most expensive fleece in the world which British people have come to adore.

Is an alpaca the same as a llama?

Alpacas and llamas belong to the same family as camels and dromedaries: the camelids. This animal family originated in North America 45 millions years ago and split into several different species. Llamas and alpacas are two of them. They are most commonly found in South America although 60,000 alpacas currently live in the UK. They are very similar and often mistaken to be the same animal. Both animals are known to spit to warn an agressor away.
